Welcome to Golf Instructions

This webpage describes all the key instructions around golf, including the important steps to take when you arrive at the course so you can have as much fun as possible.

Before you tee off, it helps to know a few key golf terms and definitions.

What to do when you get to the golf course

When you arrive at the golf course, start by checking in at the pro shop, warming up at the driving range or practice green, and making sure you're ready to tee off on time.

Key Features on the Golf Course

The golf course is made up of many parts like the tee box, fairway, rough, bunkers, and green. Understanding these areas will help you plan better shots and play more confidently.

Golf Terms Every Beginner Should Know:

  • Fairway

    The short, neatly mowed area between the tee box and the green.

  • Rough

    Longer, thicker grass beside the fairway. Harder to hit from.

  • Tee Box

    Flat area at the start of a hole where the first shot is made.

  • Bunker

    Sand-filled hollow. Escaping it needs a special swing technique.

  • Green

    Very short grass around the hole where you putt.

  • Handicap

    A number that shows how good you are, used to make games fair.

  • Par

    Expected number of strokes to finish a hole or round.

  • Birdie, Eagle, Albatross

    Scoring terms: Birdie = 1 under par, Eagle = 2 under, Albatross = 3 under.

  • Bogey, Double Bogey, Triple Bogey

    Scoring terms for going over par: 1, 2, or 3 extra strokes.
